<p><p>Newer-generation hemodynamic monitoring systems are categorized into minimally invasive and non-invasive systems. Minimally invasive hemodynamic monitoring systems involve small catheter placements to obtain precise cardiovascular data, offering real-time insights into a patient’s condition while minimizing discomfort. In contrast, non-invasive hemodynamic monitoring systems utilize external sensors and technologies, such as wearable devices, to assess hemodynamic parameters without the need for any surgical intervention. Both types enhance patient care by providing accurate and timely information for better clinical decision-making.</p></p>
